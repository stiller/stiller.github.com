Previously

After owning and operating a webhosting company for ten years, I decided I preferred building things. So after learning some Ruby I started over as a developer. After a couple of years, I joined Blendle as their fifth developer. I helped create and scale their micro-transaction system, which processess tens of milions of transactions per year.

After about a year I was in charge of running the core backend service and responsible for migrating our systems to Amazon Webservices. After a brief stint as part of the datascience team, I decided to move on. After two years seeing the company grow from a handful to 70 employees, startup life was taking its toll on family time.

Currently

I'm helping two companies getting new products out of the door. For Leaplines, I'm currently building a new authoring tool in React. For Cashrocket, I'm creating an MVP using mostly Rails and plain Javascript. I help both manage their infrastructure using things like Docker. I'm spending some of my spare time on creating a cool new approach to application development (more soon).

I like challenging, complex projects. I prefer data-driven projects and creating tools, but I don't shy away from the odd UI challenge.
